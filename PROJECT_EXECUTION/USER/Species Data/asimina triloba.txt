Asimina triloba, the papaw, pawpaw, paw paw, or paw-paw, among many regional names, is a small deciduous tree native to the eastern United States and Canada, producing a large, yellowish-green to brown fruit.
It belongs to the genus Asimina in the same plant family as the custard-apple, cherimoya, sweetsop, ylang-ylang and soursop.
The pawpaw is a patch-forming understory tree found in well-drained, deep, fertile bottom-land and hilly upland habitat, with large, simple leaves. 
Pawpaw fruits are the largest edible fruit indigenous to the United States
Pawpaw fruits have a sweet, custardish flavor somewhat similar to banana, mango, and pineapple, and are commonly eaten raw, but are also used to make ice cream and baked desserts. 
The fruit has been documented to contain the neurotoxin annonacin, which makes a potential risks of neurodegeneration.
