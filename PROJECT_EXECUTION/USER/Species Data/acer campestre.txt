Acer campestre, known as the field maple, is a flowering plant species in the family Sapindaceae. 
It is native to much of continental Europe, Britain, southwest Asia from Turkey to the Caucasus, and north Africa in the Atlas Mountains. 
It has been widely planted, and is introduced outside its native range in Europe and areas of USA and Western Australia with suitable climate
The two varieties, not accepted as distinct by all authorities, 
Acer campestre var. campestre - downy fruit
Acer campestre var. leiocarpum (Opiz) Wallr. 
(syn. A. campestre subsp. leiocarpum) - hairless fruit
The closely related Acer miyabei replaces it in eastern Asia.
